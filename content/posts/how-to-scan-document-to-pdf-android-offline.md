You're at the airport, the bank, a government office, or in the middle of a meeting. You need to scan a paper document and email it as a PDF. Right now. No Wi-Fi, no time to install something complicated, and definitely no time to fight a paywall.

This is the moment most scanner apps fail you. Either they require login, demand a subscription, watermark your scan, or simply refuse to work without internet.

Here's how to do it properly — offline, free, in under 60 seconds.

## Quick answer

Open **[Scanvo](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.nexbrothers.scanvo)**, tap **Scan Document**, point at your paper, tap capture, adjust the corners if needed, and tap **Crop & Save**. Then tap **Export → PDF**. Done. No internet required at any step.

## Why offline scanning matters

Most popular scanner apps secretly need an internet connection for at least one critical step:

- **CamScanner** uploads images to its servers for "cloud sync" by default
- **Adobe Scan** requires an Adobe account login
- **Microsoft Lens** uses your OneDrive
- **Most "free" scanners** push ads that need network access to load

If you're scanning a sensitive document — a contract, an ID card, a medical report, a tax form — you almost certainly don't want a copy of it sitting on someone else's server. And if you're somewhere with bad signal, half these apps just won't work at all.

A proper offline scanner solves both problems.

## Step-by-step guide

### Step 1: Install Scanvo while you have connectivity

Get **Scanvo** from the Play Store the next time you're online. It's small (under 30 MB), free, and has no ads. Once installed, it never needs internet again.

[Download Scanvo on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.nexbrothers.scanvo)

### Step 2: Open Scan Document mode

From the Scanvo home screen, tap **Scan Document**. The camera opens with auto edge detection running in real time. As you point your phone at a document, you'll see Scanvo highlight the corners of the paper automatically.

### Step 3: Position the document and capture

Place the document on a flat surface with reasonable contrast against the paper:

- Wood desk = good
- Dark cloth = better
- White table = bad (low contrast)

Hold your phone roughly 12 inches above the paper, parallel to the surface. Wait for the corner detection to lock on (about half a second), then tap the capture button.

### Step 4: Adjust the crop if needed

Scanvo takes you to the crop screen. The auto-detected corners are usually accurate, but if anything is slightly off, drag any corner to fix it. You can also drag the **edges** of the rectangle to push them in or out without disturbing the corners — useful when only one side needs to move.

If you have a heavily creased or folded document, tap **Reset** and adjust manually.

When the crop looks right, tap **Crop & Save**.

### Step 5: Edit and enhance

After saving, Scanvo opens the editor automatically. Here you can:

- Pick a **filter** (try "Best" for crisp text on a white background)
- Rotate or flip the image
- Adjust brightness, contrast, or saturation
- Re-crop if needed
- Add a signature (under the Sign tab)

When you're happy, tap **Save**. The document is now in your library.

### Step 6: Export as PDF

From the document view, tap **Export**. Choose **PDF** format. Optionally:

- Select page size (A4, letter, etc.)
- Add a password (for sensitive documents)
- Choose output quality

Tap **Save** or **Share**. You can email the PDF, send it via WhatsApp, attach it to a form, or save it locally — all without uploading anything.

## Pro tips for cleaner scans

- **Light from the side**, not from above. Overhead light creates a shadow under your phone.
- **Avoid flash** unless the room is genuinely dark. Flash makes paper glare and washes out text.
- **For multi-page documents**, after saving the first page tap **Add Page** in the document view. You can capture additional pages and they'll all combine into one PDF on export.
- **For receipts**, scan against a dark background and use the **Best** filter — it does an exceptional job cleaning up thermal-printed receipts.
- **For handwritten notes**, use the **Sharpen** filter to make pencil and ink stand out.
