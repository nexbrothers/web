## Why most scanner apps fail at this

Aadhaar cards are uniquely tricky:

- The card is glossy, so most apps capture glare and reflections
- The size is non-standard (smaller than A4), so auto-crop usually fails
- Two sides need to be combined into one PDF — most apps treat each capture as a separate document
- Privacy is a real concern. Aadhaar contains your biometric ID — you don't want it sitting on a server in another country

A scanner like CamScanner uploads images to its servers by default. That's a hard "no" for any Indian government document.

### Step 2: Open ID Card mode

From the Scanvo home screen, tap **ID Card**. This is a special mode designed specifically for credit-card sized documents like Aadhaar, PAN, Voter ID, and Driving Licence.

You'll see a camera view with a rounded rectangle in the centre — that's your alignment guide.

### Step 3: Position the front side inside the frame

Place your Aadhaar card on a flat, dark surface (a wooden desk works well — black paper is even better). Hold your phone parallel to the card and align the front side inside the frame. The frame is sized to match the standard ID card aspect ratio.

A few tips for a clean scan:

- Avoid direct overhead light — it causes glare on the laminated card
- Natural side-light from a window works best
- Hold steady for half a second before tapping the capture button

### Step 4: Tap capture

Scanvo will capture only the area inside the frame — not the entire camera view. This means there's no need to crop afterwards. The card itself becomes the entire image.

### Step 5: Review and tap Next

You'll see the captured front side. If it looks good, tap **Next**. If the card was slightly misaligned, tap **Crop** to fine-tune the corners — but in most cases the frame capture is enough.

### Step 6: Capture the back side

Flip the Aadhaar card over and repeat. The same frame guide appears for "Step 2/2: Capture BACK side." Capture and tap **Next** again.

### Step 7: Save

Scanvo combines the front and back into a single document and asks you to save. Tap **Save** — you now have a multi-page document containing both sides.

### Step 8: Export as PDF

Open the saved document, tap **Export**, choose **PDF format**, and tap **Save** or **Share**. You can email it, attach it to a college portal, or upload it directly to whatever government form needs it.

The text on the card stays sharp because Scanvo applies an enhancement filter automatically. The PDF is a single file with both sides, which is exactly what most forms ask for.

## Pro tips for Aadhaar scanning

- **Use a dark background.** A white background confuses auto-detection because the Aadhaar card is mostly white. Wood, dark cloth, or a black notebook gives the best edge contrast.
- **Turn off the camera flash.** Flash creates harsh hotspots on the laminated surface. Scanvo's enhancement handles low light fine.
- **Scan in landscape orientation.** Aadhaar cards are wider than they are tall, so landscape gives you more pixels per side.
- **Don't crop too tight.** Leave a 2-3 mm margin around the card so the auto-enhance has room to work without clipping the edges of the text.
