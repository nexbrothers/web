## Why receipt scanning is uniquely hard

Receipts have problems no other document has:

- **Thermal printing fades fast** — within months you can lose half the print
- **Tiny print, often crooked**, hard to read even when fresh
- **Curl up, crumple, and stick together**
- **Different sizes** — supermarket receipts are long and thin, bank receipts are short and square
- **Often have logos and ads** that confuse OCR
- **You have hundreds of them** every tax year

A scanner app needs to handle all of this gracefully. The key features: aggressive shadow/contrast cleanup (Best filter), fast batch capture, and OCR for searchability.

### Step 2: Sort receipts before scanning

Don't scan random receipts in random order. Sort them first:

- **By month**: this is what most accounting software wants
- **By category**: meals, travel, office supplies, equipment, software, etc.
- **By project / client**: useful for freelancers billing on multiple jobs

A 30-second sort saves you 30 minutes of re-organising later.

### Step 3: Set up a clean scanning surface

Receipts need contrast against the background. The best surface is:

- **A dark wooden desk** (good contrast for white receipts)
- **A black piece of paper or cloth** (best contrast)
- **NOT a white desk** — auto edge detection fails

Lay 3-5 receipts at a time, separated by an inch of space. This lets you scan them one after another without resetting between each.

### Step 4: Open Scan Document mode
From the Scanvo home screen, tap **Scan Document**.

### Step 5: Capture the first receipt

Hold your phone parallel to the surface, around 8-12 inches above the receipt. For long thermal receipts (the kind that scroll out of supermarket printers), you might need to be further away — say 15-18 inches — to fit the whole thing in frame.

Wait for auto edge detection to lock on to the receipt outline. Tap capture.

### Step 6: Crop and apply the Best filter

On the crop screen, the auto-detected corners are usually accurate for thermal receipts on a dark background. If not, drag corners to fix.

Tap **Crop & Save**. The editor opens.

In the **Filters** tab, select **Best**. This is critical for receipts:

- It removes the uneven background brightness from the thermal paper
- It darkens the faint print so it becomes readable
- It eliminates shadows from the curl of the receipt
- It makes faded receipts surprisingly legible

### Step 7: Rename the receipt with a meaningful title

Tap the document title at the top and rename it. Use a consistent format:

- `2026-04-11 Starbucks Lunch 245 INR`
- `2026-04-11 Uber Airport 1200 INR`
- `2026-03-28 Office Supplies Stationery 580 INR`

This naming convention sorts chronologically and tells you everything you need to know without opening the file. You'll thank yourself at tax time.

### Step 8: Run OCR for searchability

Tap **OCR** in the document view. Scanvo extracts the text from the receipt using Google ML Kit. Now you can search for "Uber" or "Starbucks" or "1200 INR" from the home screen and find the receipt instantly.

OCR on thermal receipts is around 85-95% accurate after Best filter — good enough for search.

### Step 9: Repeat for every receipt

Each receipt takes about 30-45 seconds end-to-end. For 50 receipts, that's 30-40 minutes. Put on a podcast and power through.

### Step 10: Export by category or month

When tax time comes, group your receipts:

- **By month**: open each receipt, tap **Export → PDF**, and save them all in a folder named `Receipts_April_2026`
- **By category**: use Scanvo's **Tools → Merge PDF** to combine all "Office Supplies" receipts into one PDF, all "Travel" into another, etc.

You now have organised, searchable, backed-up receipts ready for your accountant or tax filing software.

## Pro tips for receipt scanning

- **Scan immediately after the transaction**, before the thermal print fades. Some receipts become unreadable within 6-12 months.
- **Take a phone photo at the moment of purchase** as a quick capture, then formally scan it with Scanvo when you get home.
- **For very long receipts** (supermarket, hotel), scan in two halves and merge later. Or hold the phone further away.
- **Folded receipts**: flatten under a heavy book for 30 seconds before scanning.
- **Tape sticky thermal receipts to a white sheet** to keep them flat and prevent curling.
- **For very faded receipts**: apply Best, then bump Brightness -20 and Contrast +30 to extract every last drop of legibility.
- **Back up your tax folder to Google Drive** every month — Scanvo is offline, but you can export the PDFs and sync them to a cloud of your choice.

## Frequently asked questions

**Will the tax department accept phone scans of receipts?**
Yes, in most jurisdictions. Tax authorities accept clear, legible digital copies of original paper receipts. Keep the originals for as long as your local law requires (typically 3-7 years), even after digitising.

**Is it legal to throw away the paper receipts after scanning?**
This depends on your country. In India, the GST law allows digital copies but requires you to retain originals for some categories. In the US, the IRS accepts digital copies as long as they're legible. Check your local rules.

**How long does receipt OCR take?**
1-2 seconds per receipt on a modern phone. For 100 receipts, that's a few minutes total — fast enough that you can OCR everything in one session.

**Will Scanvo upload my receipts anywhere?**
No. Every step is offline. Your financial records stay on your phone.

**How much storage do 100 receipts take?**
After Best filter and Medium quality export, around 50-200 KB per receipt. 100 receipts = 5-20 MB. Trivial for any modern phone.

**Can I share scanned receipts with my accountant?**
Yes. Use **Tools → Merge PDF** to combine all the receipts for a month into one PDF, then share via email, WhatsApp, or any other app. Your accountant gets one clean file instead of 50 attachments.

**What if my receipt is smaller than the camera frame can detect?**
Use Scanvo's manual crop feature. Capture the wider area first, then drag corners on the crop screen to tightly frame the receipt.
