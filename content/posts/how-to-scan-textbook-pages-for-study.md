## Why textbook scanning is hard

Textbook pages have a few quirks:

- **Curved spine**: pages near the spine are slightly tilted; perspective correction is needed
- **Dense text**: requires high resolution and sharp focus or text becomes unreadable
- **Long sessions**: scanning 30 pages by hand causes fatigue → motion blur
- **File size**: a 50-page textbook chapter at high quality can hit 50+ MB
- **Two-page spreads**: you usually want both pages of an open book in one shot

A scanner that handles perspective correction, has good filters, and supports multi-page batches solves most of this.

### Step 2: Set up a stable scanning rig

For long textbook sessions, the single biggest improvement is **stability**. Your hands will shake after 5 pages. Options:

- **Phone tripod** (around $5-10 on Amazon) — best option
- **Stack of books** with your phone propped against a wall — free, works fine
- **Phone holder clamped to a desk** — also cheap

The goal: your phone should be at a fixed height (about 12-15 inches above the textbook) and you should never have to hold it.

### Step 3: Position lighting

Textbook scanning needs **even, bright, diffused light**:

- Daytime near a window is ideal
- For indoor, use **two desk lamps from opposite sides** (not directly overhead)
- Avoid the camera flash — it creates harsh hotspots and shadows from the spine
- Avoid one strong light from one side — it shadows the opposite half of the page

### Step 4: Open Scan Document mode
From the Scanvo home screen, tap **Scan Document**. The auto edge detection runs in real time.

### Step 5: Scan the first page (or spread)

Decide whether you want **one page per scan** or **both pages of the spread** in each scan. For most textbooks, scanning each page individually gives sharper results because the spine curve is less of a problem.

Position the open textbook flat under your phone. Use a glass paperweight or two heavy objects to keep both pages flat — textbooks spring back.

Wait for edge detection to lock on to the page outline. Tap capture.

### Step 6: Adjust the crop
On the crop screen, ensure the corners hug the page edge tightly. For pages near the spine, the inside corners may need to be pulled in slightly to avoid the spine shadow.

Tap **Crop & Save**.

### Step 7: Add the next page

After saving, you'll be on the document edit screen. Don't exit — instead, tap the back arrow to go to the document view, then tap **Add Page**. The camera reopens for the next scan.

Capture, adjust, save. Repeat for every page in your chapter.

For a 30-page chapter, this takes 5-10 minutes once you have a rhythm.

### Step 8: Apply the Best filter to all pages
Once all pages are scanned, open the document. For each page, open the editor and apply the **Best** filter. Best is ideal for printed textbook text — it normalises lighting, removes shadows, and makes text crisp.

(In a future Scanvo update, we'll add bulk filter application across all pages. For now, do them one at a time.)

### Step 9: Save and export
Tap **Export → PDF**. Settings:
- **Page size**: A4
- **Quality**: High (for studying, you want sharp text)
- **Margins**: Small or None

The resulting PDF will be 5-30 MB depending on chapter length.

### Step 10: (Optional) OCR for searchable text
If you'll be referencing the chapter often and need to search for specific terms, run OCR. From the document view, tap **OCR**. Scanvo extracts the text from each page using Google ML Kit. Now you can search the document by content from your home screen.

## Pro tips for textbook scanning

- **Number your pages**: even though Scanvo preserves order, label your final PDF with the chapter number and page range so you can find it later
- **Take a 30-second break every 10 pages**: stretches your hands and stops you rushing
- **Skip the index and TOC** if you don't need them — saves time and file size
- **For maths textbooks**: scan in colour, not black-and-white. Equations and diagrams need their colour preserved.
- **For old/yellowed pages**: bump **Brightness** by +10 in the Adjust tab after applying Best
- **For glossy textbook pages**: use side-light, never overhead, to avoid glare on the lamination

## Frequently asked questions

**Is it legal to scan textbook pages?**
Personal use scans for studying are generally fine under fair use / fair dealing in most countries. Distributing copyrighted scans publicly is not. Consult your local copyright law for specifics.

**How long does scanning a 30-page chapter take?**
About 10-15 minutes once you have a stable rig and rhythm. The first time will feel slower; you'll speed up.

**Can I scan two pages of a spread at once?**
Yes, but for textbooks the spine curve creates slight perspective distortion. For best quality, scan each page individually. For convenience, scanning spreads is fine.

**Will the OCR work on textbook pages?**
For printed text, yes — usually 95%+ accurate. For pages with lots of equations or diagrams, OCR will skip those (they're not text). For tables, OCR will extract the cell contents but lose the table structure.

**My PDF is huge. How do I make it smaller?**
Use **Tools → Compress PDF** with the Medium setting. A 30 MB textbook chapter typically becomes 8-12 MB without visible quality loss.

**Can Scanvo handle 100+ page batches?**
Yes, but RAM-wise it gets heavy on older phones. For very long batches (say a whole book), split it into 50-page sections and merge later with **Tools → Merge PDF**.

**Will my textbook scans be uploaded anywhere?**
No. Scanvo is fully offline. Your scans stay on your phone.
