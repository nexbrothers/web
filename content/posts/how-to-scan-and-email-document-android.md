You're at the airport, the doctor's office, your bank, or your kid's school — and someone says "can you email this document to me?" You need to scan it and send it from your phone, right now. No printer, no laptop, no desktop scanner.

This is the most common scanner-app use case in the world, and it should take 60 seconds. Here's how to do it without fighting your phone.

## Quick answer

Open **[Scanvo](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.nexbrothers.scanvo)**, tap **Scan Document**, capture the document, tap **Crop & Save**, then tap **Export → PDF → Share → Gmail**. Type the recipient and send. The whole flow takes less than a minute.

## Step-by-step guide

### Step 1: Install Scanvo (do this in advance, while you have Wi-Fi)
Get **Scanvo** from the Play Store. It's small (~30 MB), free, and offline. Once installed, you'll never need internet for the app itself.

[Download Scanvo on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.nexbrothers.scanvo)

### Step 2: Open Scan Document mode
On the Scanvo home screen, tap **Scan Document**. The camera opens with auto edge detection running.

### Step 3: Position and capture

Place the document on a flat surface with reasonable contrast (a desk, a chair, even your lap if you're standing — anywhere flat). Hold your phone parallel to the document, around 12 inches above. Wait for Scanvo to detect the corners (about half a second), then tap capture.

### Step 4: Adjust the crop if needed

The crop screen opens with auto-detected corners. If the corners are right (usually they are), tap **Crop & Save**. If not, drag corners or edges to fix.

### Step 5: (Optional) Apply a filter

After saving, the editor opens. For a clean printed document, the **Best** filter makes the text crisper and the background uniform. For a photo or coloured document, use **Original** or **Auto**. Tap **Save**.

### Step 6: Export as PDF

From the document view, tap **Export**. Choose **PDF**. Settings:
- **Page size**: A4 (default works for most cases)
- **Quality**: Medium (good balance of size and clarity)

Tap **Save**. Scanvo creates the PDF.

### Step 7: Share to email

Now tap **Share**. Your phone's share sheet opens with all the apps that can receive a file. Tap **Gmail** (or Outlook, or your default mail app).

A new email composes with the PDF already attached. Type the recipient's email, write a one-line message ("Here's the document we discussed"), and tap Send.

Total time: 45-60 seconds.

## Pro tips for fast scanning workflows

- **Know your filters**: for printed text, use **Best**; for photos, use **Original**; for handwritten notes, use **Sharpen**. Knowing this in advance saves you 10 seconds per scan.
- **Practice the gesture**: capture → crop → save → share. Do it twice when you're not in a hurry. The muscle memory makes you fast under pressure.
- **Set Gmail as your default email app**: in Android Settings → Apps → Default apps → Email. This makes the share sheet faster.
- **Keep your phone's clipboard ready**: if you're emailing the same recipient multiple times, copy their email address once and paste each time.
- **For multi-page documents**: after scanning the first page, tap **Add Page** in the document view to add more before exporting.
- **For very simple use cases**: you can also share a JPG instead of a PDF — Scanvo's **Share → Image** option skips the PDF step entirely. Faster for casual sharing.

## Frequently asked questions

**Why do you recommend PDF instead of just sharing a JPG?**
PDFs are universally accepted by office portals, government forms, and business email systems. JPGs are sometimes rejected by formal portals because they're easier to alter. For casual sharing (between friends, family), JPG is fine.

**What if I don't have Gmail / Outlook?**
Any email app works — Yahoo Mail, ProtonMail, your phone's default mail app. The share sheet shows whatever's installed.

**Can I send the document via WhatsApp instead of email?**
Yes — when you tap Share, WhatsApp appears in the share sheet. PDFs send fine via WhatsApp.

**Will the recipient need a special app to open the PDF?**
No. PDFs open in any phone or computer that has a basic file viewer. Every modern phone, every email client, every browser.

**Will Scanvo upload my document anywhere?**
No. Every step happens on your device. The only network activity is when you actually share via Gmail, WhatsApp, etc. — and at that point, it's your sharing action, not Scanvo's.

**What if I want to email a document I already have on my phone?**
You don't need Scanvo for that — just open the file in your file manager and tap Share → Gmail. Scanvo is for when you need to scan a paper document first.

**Can I edit the document before sharing?**
Yes. Scanvo's editor lets you crop, rotate, apply filters, sign, and adjust before exporting. All edits happen offline and in seconds.

**Is there a faster workflow for scanning the same kind of document repeatedly?**
For repetitive scanning (e.g. scanning 50 receipts in one sitting), use **Scan Document** with **Add Page** between each scan. All your scans go into one document, which you can then export as a single PDF.
