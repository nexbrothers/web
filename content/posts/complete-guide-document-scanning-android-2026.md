### Must-have features

- **Auto edge detection** — manually adjusting corners every scan is exhausting

- **Filters** for cleaning up scans (especially shadow removal and contrast boost)
- **Multi-page documents** — combine many pages into one PDF
- **PDF export** with reasonable file size
- **Works offline** — Wi-Fi is unreliable when you actually need to scan
- **No watermark** in the free tier
- **No ads** that interrupt your workflow

### Nice-to-haves

- OCR for searchable text
- Cloud sync (optional, off by default)
- ID card mode for credit-card sized documents

- **Forced cloud upload** — if scans go to a server by default, it's a privacy red flag
- **Watermarks in the free tier** — usually means the app is trying to pressure you into a subscription
- **Login required** — adds friction with no benefit

## Part 2: Setting up for clean scans

Software can clean up a lot — but the cleaner the source image, the better the final scan. Spend 30 seconds on the setup and save 5 minutes of editing later.

### Lighting

- **Diffused side-light is best** — daytime near a window, or a desk lamp angled from the side
- **Avoid overhead light** — creates the shadow of your phone on the document
- **Avoid the camera flash** — creates harsh hotspots on glossy paper
- **Two side-lamps** are even better than one (cancels out shadows)

### Surface

- **Flat and rigid** — the document should not curl
- **Contrast against the document** — for white paper, use a dark wooden desk; for cream paper, use white
- **Clean and clutter-free** — auto edge detection works best when nothing else is in the camera view

### Phone position

- **Parallel to the document** — perpendicular gives the cleanest perspective
- **12-15 inches above** for most documents
- **Hold steady** — let your hand settle for half a second before tapping capture
- **For long sessions, use a tripod or a stack of books** as a stand

### Document prep

- **Flatten the page** with a glass paperweight or two heavy books if it's curled
- **Remove staples** if you're scanning multiple pages
- **For bound notebooks**, open as flat as the spine allows

## Part 6: Common mistakes (and how to avoid them)

### Mistake 1: Using the wrong filter
- **Problem**: applying the Best filter to handwritten notes erases pencil marks
- **Fix**: use Sharpen or Auto for handwriting; Best is only for printed text

### Mistake 2: Scanning in poor lighting
- **Problem**: scans look dark, washed out, or shadowed
- **Fix**: use diffused side-light, never overhead, never flash

### Mistake 3: Holding the phone at an angle
- **Problem**: trapezoidal distortion makes text harder to read
- **Fix**: hold parallel to the document; perspective correction will fix small angles automatically

### Mistake 4: Not cropping precisely
- **Problem**: extra background gets included, making the scan look unprofessional
- **Fix**: spend 5 seconds adjusting the corners on the crop screen

### Mistake 5: Saving at maximum quality always
- **Problem**: PDFs become huge, can't be emailed
- **Fix**: use Medium quality for everyday scans; save High only for important archival documents

### Mistake 6: Not running OCR
- **Problem**: scans become useless because you can't search them
- **Fix**: run OCR after scanning; takes 1-2 seconds per page and makes everything searchable

### Mistake 7: Trusting cloud-based scanners with sensitive documents
- **Problem**: your scans end up on a server in another country
- **Fix**: use a fully offline scanner for IDs, contracts, financial documents

### Mistake 8: Not labelling your scans
- **Problem**: you have 200 PDFs all named "Scan_2026-04-11"
- **Fix**: rename each scan with a meaningful title before saving

## Part 7: Organising your scans

A scanner is only useful if you can find what you scanned later. Tips for organisation:

- **Folder by category**: receipts, IDs, contracts, medical, tax, school, etc.
- **Folder by year**: combine with category for "2026/Receipts", "2026/Tax", etc.
- **Consistent naming**: `2026-04-11 Starbucks Lunch 245 INR` is better than `Receipt_1`
- **Use OCR**: makes everything searchable by content, not just filename
- **Back up monthly**: export your scanned PDFs to Google Drive, Dropbox, or a USB drive once a month
- **Delete junk**: review scans every quarter and delete what you no longer need

## Part 8: Troubleshooting

### My scans look blurry
- Hold the phone steadier
- Wait for autofocus to lock before tapping
- Don't use the camera flash
- Make sure your camera lens is clean

### Auto edge detection isn't working
- Use a darker / higher-contrast background
- Improve lighting
- Hold the phone parallel to the document
- Switch to the alternative scanner app (some apps have better detection than others)

### My PDFs are too large to email
- Use Compress PDF (Tools → Compress PDF)
- Reduce the per-image quality during export

### OCR is missing words
- Re-scan with the Best filter applied
- Improve lighting
- Make sure the source text is at least 10 pixels tall in the captured image

### The scanner app crashes on long batches
- Save and exit between every 20-30 pages
- Restart the app to free memory
- Update the app to the latest version
