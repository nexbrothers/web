## Why scanning a PAN card is annoying

PAN cards have several quirks that confuse generic scanner apps:

- **Small size** (the standard ID card is 85.6 × 54 mm) — auto-crop usually fails
- **Glossy laminated finish** — produces glare under direct light
- **Thin black border** — confuses edge detection on dark backgrounds
- **Two sides** — most apps treat each capture as a separate document
- **Tax department portal limits** — they often require under 500 KB or 1 MB

A proper ID card mode solves all of this. Scanvo crops only the area inside a guide frame, keeps both sides as one document, and produces small, clean PDFs.

## Step-by-step guide

### Step 1: Install Scanvo
Free, offline, no signup. Get it from the Play Store.

### Step 2: Open ID Card mode
From the home screen, tap **ID Card**. You'll see the camera with a rounded rectangular guide frame in the centre. The frame matches the standard credit-card aspect ratio that PAN cards use.

### Step 3: Position the front of your PAN card
Lay your PAN card flat on a dark surface (a wooden desk works; black cloth is best). Hold your phone parallel to the surface, around 8-12 inches above. Align the card inside the guide frame.

For glare-free scans:
- **Avoid direct overhead light** — it reflects off the lamination
- **Use side-lighting from a window** for daytime scans
- **Don't use flash** — it creates harsh hotspots
- **Hold steady** for half a second before tapping

### Step 4: Tap capture
Scanvo crops only the frame area, not the entire camera view. The result is a tightly framed front side with no background clutter.

### Step 5: Review and tap Next
You'll see the captured front. If it looks clean, tap **Next**. If the card was tilted, tap **Crop** to fine-tune the corners — but most of the time the frame capture is perfect.

### Step 6: Capture the back
Flip the PAN card. The frame guide reappears with "Step 2/2 : Capture BACK side." Position, tap capture, tap Next.

### Step 7: Save
Scanvo combines front and back into a single document. Tap **Save**. The document goes into your library with both pages.

### Step 8: Export as a small PDF
Open the saved document, tap **Export**, and choose **PDF**. For most government forms, set page size to **A4** and quality to **Medium** — this keeps the file under 500 KB while staying perfectly readable.

If the form has a strict size limit (say 200 KB), use **Tools → Compress PDF** afterwards and pick the **Low** quality preset. The PAN number and photo will still be sharp.

### Step 9: Share or upload
From the saved PDF, tap **Share**. Send it directly to the bank email, the government portal, or save it locally for later upload.

## Tips for cleaner PAN scans

- **Dark background**: a dark surface improves contrast around the card edges and helps the auto-crop work even when the frame guide isn't perfect
- **Diffused light**: avoid direct sun and direct lamp light — both create glare on the laminated face
- **No fingers in the shot**: most scanner apps will try to detect your fingers as part of the document; keep them off the frame area
- **Hold the phone steady**: pressing capture causes a tiny shake; press gently and hold for a fraction of a second
- **Review before sending**: zoom in on the saved PDF and confirm the PAN number is readable. If not, re-scan in better light.

**Is it safe to scan a PAN card on a phone app?**
Only if the app processes everything offline. Scanvo is fully offline — no scan ever leaves your phone. CamScanner, Adobe Scan, and Microsoft Lens all upload to their cloud by default. Avoid them for tax-sensitive documents.

**Why does the bank portal reject my upload?**
The most common reasons: file is too large (compress with Scanvo), file is in JPG instead of PDF (export as PDF in Scanvo), or only one side is visible (use ID Card mode which captures both sides automatically).

**Can I scan an e-PAN card the same way?**
e-PAN is already a PDF — you don't need to scan it. Just submit the PDF directly. If a portal demands a "scanned copy", you can open the e-PAN, screenshot it, and re-export as PDF using Scanvo's **Tools → JPG to PDF**.

**What if my PAN card is damaged or faded?**
