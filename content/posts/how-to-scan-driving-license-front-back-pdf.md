## Why scanning a DL is tricky

Driving licences (whether Indian, US, EU, or international) share a few annoying properties:

- **Laminated and glossy** — produces glare under direct light
- **Holographic strips and security features** — reflect light at specific angles
- **Dark photo on a busy background** — confuses auto-crop
- **Standard credit-card size (85.6 × 54 mm)** — too small for generic Scan Document mode auto-crop to work reliably
- **Two sides with different layouts** — front has photo, back has signature/address
- **Many forms require both sides on one PDF** — most apps make you merge manually

A dedicated ID card mode handles all of this with one workflow.

### Step 2: Open ID Card mode
From the Scanvo home screen, tap **ID Card**. The camera opens with a rounded rectangular guide frame in the centre. The frame is sized for credit-card-sized documents — driving licences fit perfectly.

### Step 3: Position the front of the licence

Place your driving licence on a dark, flat surface (a wooden desk works; black cloth or paper is best). Hold your phone parallel to the surface, around 8-12 inches above the licence. Align the licence inside the guide frame.

For glare-free scans:
- **Avoid direct overhead light** — it reflects off the laminated face
- **Use side-lighting from a window** during the day
- **Avoid the camera flash** — it creates harsh hotspots
- **Tilt the licence slightly** if there's a holographic strip — it should reflect light away from the camera, not into it

### Step 4: Tap capture
Scanvo crops only the area inside the frame. The result is a tightly cropped front side, no background, no fingers, no clutter.

### Step 5: Review and tap Next
You'll see the captured front. If clean, tap **Next**. If the licence was tilted, tap **Crop** to fine-tune the corners — but for most well-aligned captures, this isn't needed.

### Step 6: Capture the back
Flip the driving licence. The frame guide reappears with "Step 2/2 : Capture BACK side." Position the back inside the frame and tap capture.

### Step 7: Tap Next, then Save
Scanvo combines front and back into a single document. Tap **Save**. The document is now in your library with both sides as separate pages.

### Step 8: Export as PDF
Open the saved document. Tap **Export → PDF**. Settings:
- **Page size**: A4
- **Quality**: Medium (good balance for upload to portals)
- **Margins**: Small or None

The PDF is typically under 500 KB — small enough for any portal that has a file size limit.

### Step 9: Share
Tap **Share** to send the PDF directly via email, WhatsApp, or upload to a portal. Or tap **Open File** to view it in your default PDF reader.

## Pro tips for clean DL scans

- **Dark background**: improves edge contrast and helps the auto-crop work
- **Diffused side-lighting**: the single biggest improvement for laminated documents
- **Slightly tilt** the licence if it has a holographic strip that reflects glare
- **Hold steady**: don't rush the capture button — let your hand settle
- **Review the photo area**: zoom in on your photo on the front. It should be clear enough to recognise. If blurry, re-scan.
- **For very old or worn licences**: use the **Best** filter in the editor to enhance contrast and bring out faded text
