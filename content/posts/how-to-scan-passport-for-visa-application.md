## What visa offices actually want

Different embassies have slightly different rules, but most visa applications want:

- **Colour scan** (not black-and-white)
- **Bio page** (the page with your photo, name, passport number, expiry date)
- **Both pages of the data spread** visible at full resolution
- **300 DPI or higher**
- **No glare, no shadows, no fingers in the frame**
- **PDF or JPEG**, usually under 2 MB

A good phone scanner can hit every one of these requirements without paying for anything.

### Step 2: Set up a clean scanning surface

Passport scans need to look professional. Spend 30 seconds on the setup:

- Find a **flat, dark surface** (a wooden desk or dark cloth)
- Open your passport flat to the bio page
- Use a **glass paperweight** or a **small heavy book** to keep both pages flat — passports tend to spring back
- Position **near a window** for diffused natural light, or use **two side-lamps** indoors
- Avoid overhead light (creates glare on the laminated bio page)

### Step 3: Open Scan Document mode
From the Scanvo home screen, tap **Scan Document**. Don't use ID Card mode for passports — passports are larger than the standard ID frame, and you need both pages of the spread visible.

### Step 4: Position and capture
Hold your phone parallel to the passport, around 12-15 inches above. Both pages of the open passport should fit comfortably inside the camera view, with about 1 inch of margin on each side.

Wait for Scanvo's auto edge detection to lock on to the passport's outer edge. This is usually fast on a dark background.

Tap the capture button. Hold the phone still for half a second after — passport scans are zero-tolerance for motion blur because the small text on the bio page becomes unreadable.

### Step 5: Adjust the crop carefully
Scanvo opens the crop screen with auto-detected corners. For passports, this is usually 90% accurate but the corners may need a small adjustment.

- Drag corners or edges to align tightly with the outer edge of the passport
- Don't crop into the passport pages — leave 2-3 mm of dark background around the edge
- If the passport is slightly tilted, the crop will straighten it via perspective correction

When the corners are perfect, tap **Crop & Save**.

### Step 6: Apply the Best filter for clean output
After saving, Scanvo opens the editor automatically. Tap the **Filters** tab and select **Best**.

Best does three things that matter for passport scans:
- Removes shadows from uneven lighting
- Normalises the background (the laminated paper becomes uniformly bright)
- Sharpens the small text on the bio page

You'll see the difference instantly — the passport looks like it was scanned on a flatbed scanner.

### Step 7: Optionally adjust brightness and contrast
If the passport bio page has a dark photo area (a recent photo with dark clothing), bump **Contrast** by +10 in the **Adjust** tab to make the text stand out.

### Step 8: Save and export
Tap **Save** to commit the changes. Then **Export → PDF**. Set:
- **Page size**: A4
- **Quality**: High (visa offices reject low-quality scans)
- **Colour mode**: Colour (not greyscale)
- **Margins**: None or Small

The resulting PDF will typically be 1-2 MB — well within most visa office limits.

### Step 9: Verify before uploading
Open the saved PDF and zoom in on the passport number, the photo, and the name fields. Every character must be legible. If anything is blurry, re-scan with better lighting before submitting.

### Step 10: Upload or share
Tap **Share** in Scanvo and send the PDF directly to the visa portal, embassy email, or your travel agent.

## Pro tips for visa-grade passport scans

- **Time of day matters**: scan around mid-morning or mid-afternoon when daylight is bright but indirect. Midday sun creates harsh shadows. Evening light is too warm.
- **Use both pages of the spread**: even if only the bio page has the photo, scan both visible pages. Visa offices want context.
- **Don't crop into the page edge**: leave a small dark margin so reviewers can see the page is real and unaltered.
- **Keep the original photo**: if the visa portal asks for both PDF and JPG, you can also tap **Export → JPG** in Scanvo for a separate copy.
- **Scan visa pages too**: many applications want a scan of any existing visa pages. Each one is a separate page in the same document — use **Add Page** in Scanvo's document view.

## Frequently asked questions

**Will the visa office accept a phone scan?**
Yes. As long as it's high quality, in colour, and shows the full bio page clearly, phone scans are universally accepted in 2026. They've been the norm for online visa applications for years.

**What DPI does the scan need to be?**
Scanvo's default scan resolution at the recommended distance is well above 300 DPI for an A4-equivalent document size. You don't need to manually set DPI.

**Should I scan in colour or black and white?**
Colour. Always. Black-and-white passport scans are often rejected because the embassy can't verify document authenticity.

**Will Scanvo upload my passport anywhere?**
No. Every step is offline. Your passport bio page never leaves your phone.

**What about my visa pages and stamps?**
Use **Scan Document** for each one. After saving the bio page document, tap **Add Page** to add the visa pages to the same multi-page PDF.

**The page is too large for the camera frame. What now?**
Move further away from the document. If you can't, scan the page in two halves and use **Tools → Merge PDF** to combine them later.

**My passport has a holographic strip. Will it cause glare?**
Yes — and it's hard to avoid. The trick: tilt the passport slightly so the hologram reflects light away from the camera. Also use diffused side-light, never direct light.
