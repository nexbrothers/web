CamScanner used to be the default scanner app on every Android phone. Then in 2019, Kaspersky discovered it was bundling malware in updates. The app was pulled from Play Store, eventually returned, and most of the original users never came back.

In 2026, CamScanner still works, but most people are looking for something better — a scanner that doesn't upload your documents to servers in another country, doesn't require a subscription, and doesn't watermark your PDFs.

Here's an honest comparison of the best CamScanner alternatives in 2026, including the one we built ourselves.

## What to look for in a CamScanner alternative

Before comparing apps, let's agree on what matters:

- **Offline processing**: Your documents should never leave your phone. This is the biggest reason people switch from CamScanner.
- **No watermark**: Free tiers should not slap a logo on your PDFs.
- **No mandatory subscription**: Core features (scan, crop, save as PDF) should be free forever.
- **Auto edge detection**: Manually adjusting corners on every scan is exhausting.
- **OCR support**: For making scanned text searchable and copyable.
- **Multi-page documents**: Ability to combine many pages into one PDF.
- **Privacy policy**: Read it. Yes, actually read it. You'd be shocked.

## Why you should care about the corporate vs indie distinction

Big-corporate scanner apps have one job: to feed users into their broader ecosystem. Microsoft Lens exists to push OneDrive subscriptions. Adobe Scan exists to push Acrobat Pro. CamScanner exists to push CamScanner Cloud. None of them are optimised for "user wants a clean PDF, fast, locally."

Indie scanner apps don't have a backend ecosystem to push you into. They're just trying to make a good scanner. That alignment of incentives matters.

## How to migrate from CamScanner

If you're switching to Scanvo (or any other scanner), here's how to bring your existing CamScanner documents:

1. In CamScanner, open each document
2. Tap the share icon → Save as PDF → save to your phone storage
3. In Scanvo, tap **Import from Gallery** or use the **Tools → Open PDF** option
4. Re-organise the documents in Scanvo's library

For batch migration, CamScanner's "Export All" feature works but is buried in settings. Once exported, you can drag the folder of PDFs into Scanvo's import flow.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is CamScanner safe to use in 2026?**
CamScanner is technically safe — the malware incident from 2019 was patched. But it still defaults to uploading your scans to its cloud, which is hosted partly outside your country. For non-sensitive documents it's fine. For ID cards, contracts, or financial documents, it's a privacy concern.

**Do any of these alternatives have iOS versions?**
Yes — Adobe Scan, Microsoft Lens, Genius Scan, and Tiny Scanner all have iOS versions. Scanvo is currently Android-only.

**Which one has the best OCR?**
Adobe Scan and Microsoft Lens have the best OCR for English. For Indian languages, Microsoft Lens supports Hindi OCR; Scanvo currently does Latin script via Google ML Kit. For everyday OCR (turning a scan into searchable text), all of them are usable.

**Which is fastest?**
Scanvo and Tiny Scanner — both are designed to be lightweight. Adobe Scan and CamScanner have heavier UIs and are slower to launch on older phones.

**Which works best for receipts specifically?**
Scanvo's Best filter is genuinely the best at cleaning up thermal-printed receipts with shadow and curl. It uses background subtraction which most cheap scanners don't bother with.
