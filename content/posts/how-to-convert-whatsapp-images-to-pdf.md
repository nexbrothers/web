A friend WhatsApps you 8 photos of a contract, a textbook chapter, or your child's homework — and you need to send all of them as a **single PDF** to a school portal or office form. Forwarding 8 separate images won't work. Most online "image to PDF" sites either limit you to 3 free conversions or watermark the result.

Here's how to do it properly on your phone, in under a minute, completely free and offline.

## Quick answer

Open **[Scanvo](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.nexbrothers.scanvo)**, tap **Tools → JPG to PDF**, select all your WhatsApp photos from the gallery, drag to reorder if needed, and tap **Convert**. You get a single PDF with all images on separate pages, in the order you chose.

- **Slow**: 30-60 seconds per upload + processing
- **Internet required**: useless on a flaky connection

A native phone app fixes all of this. Scanvo's JPG to PDF tool processes everything locally — instant, free, no limits.

## Step-by-step guide

### Step 1: Install Scanvo
Get **Scanvo** from the Play Store. Free, no ads, no watermark, works offline.

[Download Scanvo on Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.nexbrothers.scanvo)

### Step 2: Open the JPG to PDF tool
From the Scanvo home screen, tap **Tools**. You'll see a grid of PDF utilities. Tap **JPG to PDF**.

### Step 3: Pick your WhatsApp photos
Tap the **Select Images** button. Your phone's gallery picker opens. Navigate to **WhatsApp Images** (or **WhatsApp/Media/WhatsApp Images**) and select the photos you want to combine.

You can pick multiple photos at once — tap each one to add it to the selection, then tap **Done**.

### Step 4: Reorder pages (optional)
Scanvo shows you a thumbnail strip of the selected images in selection order. To change the order, long-press an image and drag it to a new position. The order here becomes the page order in the final PDF.

### Step 5: Choose page settings
Tap the gear icon to set:

- **Page size**: A4 (default), Letter, Legal, or Fit to image
- **Page orientation**: Portrait or Landscape
- **Margin**: None, Small, Medium, Large
- **Image quality**: High (sharp, larger file) or Medium (smaller file)

For a school assignment or contract, A4 portrait with a small margin is the standard choice. For photos, Fit to image gives you a tighter PDF without empty whitespace.

### Step 6: Convert
Tap **Convert**. Scanvo creates the PDF instantly — usually under a second per image. You'll see a progress bar for very large batches.

### Step 7: Save or share
Tap **Save** to keep the PDF in your Scanvo library, or **Share** to send it directly via email, WhatsApp, Drive, or any other app on your phone. Tap **Open File** to view the PDF in your default PDF reader.

## Pro tips

- **Crop first if the images are blurry around the edges.** Scanvo has a built-in crop tool — use it before converting if your WhatsApp photos have unwanted background.
- **For text-heavy images** (e.g. screenshots of textbook pages), apply the **Best** filter in the editor first. This makes the text crisp and the PDF much smaller.
- **For receipts**, scan with **Scan Document** mode instead of importing — the auto edge detection produces a cleaner result than a raw photo.
- **Combine multiple WhatsApp groups' photos**: there's no limit on how many images you can include, so you can pull from any folder on your phone.
- **Keep the original photos** — Scanvo doesn't delete or move your WhatsApp images. The PDF is a separate file.

**How many images can I combine into one PDF?**
There's no hard limit. We've tested with 100+ images. The processing is fast and the resulting PDF size is reasonable thanks to built-in compression.

**Will the PDF have a watermark?**
No. Scanvo never adds watermarks on any tier.

Yes — in Tools → Lock PDF, you can add a password to any PDF after creation.

Yes. Scanvo handles full 12-megapixel and 50-megapixel photos. If you want a smaller file, choose "Medium" image quality during conversion.

**What about HEIC photos from iPhone friends?**
Scanvo handles HEIC and JPG. If your phone can open the file in the gallery, Scanvo can convert it.

**Can I add a cover page or table of contents?**
Currently Scanvo creates one page per image. For cover pages, scan a custom title image first and include it as the first item in the conversion list.

**Will my photos be uploaded anywhere?**
No. The conversion happens entirely on your device. Scanvo doesn't have a cloud or sync feature — your photos stay yours.
