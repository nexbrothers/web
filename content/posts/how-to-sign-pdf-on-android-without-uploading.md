You receive a contract by email. The other party needs your signature on it, today. You don't want to print, sign, scan, and re-attach. You also don't want to upload your half-signed contract to a cloud service in another country, where someone may or may not store it.

There's a better way: sign the PDF directly on your phone, offline, with your finger or stylus, in 30 seconds.

## Quick answer

Open **[Scanvo](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.nexbrothers.scanvo)**, tap **Tools → Sign PDF**, pick the PDF, draw your signature with your finger, drag it onto the page, and tap **Save**. The signed PDF stays on your phone — nothing is uploaded.

## Why offline signing matters for contracts

Most "free" PDF signing tools work like this:

1. You upload your PDF to their server
2. They process it
3. They email you a link to download the signed version

This means **your contract sits on someone else's computer**. For legal documents, NDAs, employment offers, or anything containing personal data, that's a problem. Even if the company is reputable, you're trusting their security, their data retention policies, and their jurisdiction.

A proper offline signer never uploads anything. The PDF stays on your device. You sign it. You save it. Done. No third party ever sees it.

### Step 2: Open the Sign PDF tool
From the Scanvo home screen, tap **Tools**. You'll see a grid of PDF utilities. Tap **Sign PDF**.

### Step 3: Pick the PDF you want to sign
Tap the file picker icon and choose the PDF from your phone storage. Scanvo loads it and shows you the first page.

### Step 4: Navigate to the signature page
If the PDF has multiple pages, swipe left or use the page picker at the bottom to find the page where you need to sign.

### Step 5: Draw your signature
Tap the **Sign** button. A blank canvas appears. Draw your signature with your finger or, if you have one, a stylus.

Tips for a clean signature:

- Hold your phone in landscape mode for more drawing room
- Sign slowly — phone signatures look messier than paper signatures because your finger is wider than a pen tip
- If it doesn't look right, tap **Clear** and try again
- Practice 2-3 times before saving

When you're happy, tap **Done**.

### Step 6: Place the signature on the page
Your signature appears as a movable element. Drag it to the right spot on the page. Pinch to resize. The signature has a transparent background, so it sits naturally on top of the existing content.

### Step 7: Save the signed PDF
Tap **Save**. Scanvo writes a new PDF with the signature embedded — the original is untouched. You can open it with any PDF reader and the signature appears exactly where you placed it.

### Step 8: Share or send
From the signed PDF, tap **Share**. You can email it, send it via WhatsApp, attach it to a form, or save it to cloud storage of your choice. **You** decide where it goes — Scanvo never sends it anywhere automatically.

## Pro tips for signing PDFs

- **Use a stylus if you have one.** Even a cheap capacitive stylus produces a much cleaner signature than a finger.
- **Save your signature.** Scanvo can remember your signature for next time, so you don't have to re-draw it for every contract.
- **For contracts with multiple signature spots**, sign once, then duplicate the signature element and place each copy on its own line.
- **Want a typed signature instead of a drawn one?** Type your name in cursive font in any image editor, save as a transparent PNG, and import it as a stamp.
- **Sign in landscape** for a steadier hand. The bigger canvas means smaller wrist motion → smoother strokes.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are signatures from Scanvo legally valid?**
A drawn or typed signature on a PDF is valid in most jurisdictions for ordinary contracts (this is what's called a "simple electronic signature"). For legal disputes or documents that require a *qualified electronic signature* (QES), you need a certified provider — Scanvo isn't one. For 95% of everyday signing — vendor agreements, NDAs, freelance contracts, school forms, rental forms — a Scanvo signature is fine.

**Does this work for handwritten initials?**
Yes. You can save multiple signatures (full signature + initials) and place each one separately.

**Can I sign multiple pages at once?**
Currently you place signatures one page at a time. For long contracts that need a signature on every page, plan to spend 1-2 minutes per signature.

**Does the PDF size increase a lot after signing?**
A signature adds typically 20-100 KB to the PDF — so a 1 MB contract becomes ~1.05 MB. Negligible.

**Can I sign PDFs that are already password-protected?**
No, you'll need to remove the password first (in Tools → Unlock PDF, if you know the password) and then sign.

**Does Scanvo upload my PDF anywhere when signing?**
No. Every step happens on your phone. Network access is not required to sign. You can put your phone in airplane mode and the entire flow still works.

**What if I make a mistake after saving?**
The original PDF is never modified. If the signed version is wrong, just delete it and start over from the original.
