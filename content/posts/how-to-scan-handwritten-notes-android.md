## Why handwritten notes need special treatment

Handwritten notes have problems that printed documents don't:

- **Low contrast**: pencil and light pen ink fade against the page colour
- **Imperfect lines**: paper isn't always straight, ink isn't always dark
- **Coloured highlights**: most filters destroy them by converting to black and white
- **Variable handwriting**: clean block letters scan well; cursive scribbles don't
- **Page texture**: lined or grid paper can interfere with text recognition

A scanner app that's tuned for printed documents will often **erase parts of your handwriting** while trying to "clean up" the page. You need different settings.

### Step 2: Set up a clean scanning environment

Handwritten pages are sensitive to lighting:

- Use a **bright, evenly lit space** — daytime near a window is ideal
- Avoid the camera flash — it creates hard shadows on the texture of the page
- Lay the notebook **flat and open**, with both pages of the spread visible if you want them in one shot
- Use a **white desk** for white paper (high contrast against the page edge), or **dark cloth** for cream/yellow paper

### Step 3: Open Scan Document mode
From the home screen, tap **Scan Document**. Don't use ID Card mode — handwritten notebook pages are larger than the ID frame.

### Step 4: Capture each page
Hold your phone parallel to the page, around 12 inches above. Wait for auto edge detection to lock on. Tap capture.

For multi-page notebooks:
- Capture page 1, tap **Add Page** in the next screen
- Capture page 2, tap **Add Page**
- Repeat for as many pages as you have

This creates a single multi-page PDF instead of individual scans. Much easier to share later.

### Step 5: Adjust the crop
For each page, the crop screen will show auto-detected corners. For notebook pages with curved spines, pull the inside edge slightly inward to avoid the dark spine line. For flat A4 pages, the auto-crop is usually fine.

Tap **Crop & Save** for each page.

### Step 6: Apply the right filter — NOT Best

This is the most important step. Open the editor and tap **Filters**. Try these in order:

1. **Sharpen** — best for handwritten notes. Boosts the contrast of pen/pencil strokes without destroying lines or coloured highlights.
2. **Auto** — gentle enhancement. Use for clean handwriting on white paper.
3. **Original** — no filter at all. Use if your notes already look clear and you don't want to risk artefacts.

**Avoid the Best filter for handwritten notes.** Best is designed for printed text on uniform white backgrounds. It uses background subtraction which can erase faint pencil marks.

### Step 7: Adjust brightness and contrast
After picking a filter, switch to the **Adjust** tab:

- For pencil notes: **Brightness -10**, **Contrast +20** (makes pencil stand out)
- For pen notes: **Brightness 0**, **Contrast +10** (slight pop)
- For coloured highlighter: **Saturation +20** (preserves highlight colours)

### Step 8: Save and export
Tap **Save** to commit changes. Then **Export → PDF**. For notes:
- **Page size**: A4
- **Quality**: High (you want to read these later)
- **Margins**: Small

The PDF preserves all your pages in order.

### Step 9: (Optional) Run OCR for searchability
If your handwriting is reasonably neat (block letters, not cursive), tap **OCR** in the document view. Scanvo extracts the text using Google ML Kit.

**Important**: OCR for handwriting is around 60-80% accurate even for clean writing. Always proofread the extracted text before using it for anything important. For cursive or messy handwriting, OCR is mostly useless — stick with the image-based PDF.

## Pro tips for note scanning

- **Be consistent with the angle**: hold your phone at the same height for every page. Mismatched scales make the final PDF feel chaotic.
- **Use a phone tripod or a propped-up stack of books** for long note scanning sessions. Your hands will get tired and start shaking.
- **Scan as you go**: don't let pages pile up for weeks. Scan the same day. Pages get crinkled, ink fades, and some get lost.
- **Number your pages by hand** before scanning. This is a basic discipline that pays off when you're trying to find a specific section in a 50-page PDF later.
- **For maths and science notes**: scan in colour, not black and white. Coloured equations and diagrams need their colours preserved.

## Frequently asked questions

**Why is the Best filter bad for handwritten notes?**
Best uses background subtraction to remove uneven lighting and force the background to white. This is great for printed text, but for handwriting it can erase faint pencil marks and tone down coloured highlighters. Use **Sharpen** or **Auto** instead.

**Can OCR really read my handwriting?**
For clean block letters, yes — usually 70-85% accurate. For cursive, mixed scripts, or messy handwriting, OCR is unreliable. Don't trust it without proofreading.

**My pages are wrinkled and curled. Will the scan look bad?**
Use a glass paperweight or a heavy book to flatten the page before scanning. If the wrinkles are permanent, you can't fix them with software — but the scan will still be readable.

**Can I scan a notebook spread (two pages at once)?**
Yes, if both pages fit comfortably in the camera view. For most notebooks (A5-A4 size), this works. For larger notebooks, scan each page separately and combine them with **Add Page**.

**Will Scanvo upload my notes anywhere?**
No. Every step is offline. Your notes stay on your phone.

**How big does the PDF get?**
A 20-page notes PDF at High quality is typically 5-15 MB. If you need it smaller, use **Tools → Compress PDF** afterwards.
